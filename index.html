FAQ

Cooperative Society Information

Frequently asked questions about our cooperative society, membership, and benefits.

What is a cooperative society?

A cooperative society is an autonomous association of persons united voluntarily to meet their common economic, social, and cultural needs and aspirations through a jointly-owned and democratically-controlled enterprise.

How do I become a member?

To become a member, you need to fill out the membership application form and pay the required membership fee. You can find more details on our membership page.

What are the benefits of membership?

Members enjoy various benefits including profit sharing, voting rights, access to exclusive services, and the opportunity to participate in the decision-making process of the cooperative.

How can I participate in decision-making?

Members can participate in decision-making by attending general meetings, voting on important issues, and being elected to the board of directors or other committees.

What services does the cooperative offer?

Our cooperative offers a range of services including financial assistance, educational programs, community events, and more. Please visit our services page for a complete list.

How are profits distributed?

Profits are distributed among members based on their level of participation in the cooperative. This can include dividends, patronage refunds, or reinvestment into the cooperative's services and infrastructure.

Can I volunteer with the cooperative?

Yes, we welcome volunteers! You can get involved by joining one of our committees, helping with events, or offering your skills and expertise. Please contact us for more information on volunteer opportunities.

How do I contact the cooperative?

You can contact us via email, phone, or by visiting our office. Our contact details are available on the contact page of our website.
